    NOW & THEN

    The story of Hanoi “now and then” is not only in the minds of Vietnamese people but also has created interesting memories for foreign visitors. They may be tourists who visited many years ago and now have a chance to return or expatriates who have lived and worked in Hanoi or other parts of Vietnam for a long time. All of them have diﬀerent memories and impressions of this wonderful land. Along with international integration, FDI ﬂow into Vietnam increases year on year. The number of foreigners choosing to live, work and invest in Vietnam is also increasing. According to Ms. Le Hoang Chau – President of HCMC’s Real Estate Association (HOREA), “In 2015, Ho Chi Minh City alone hosted 30,000 foreign general managers and senior experts of international companies. There were 80,000 Koreans, 8,000 Japanese and 1,200 Germans living and working across Vietnam. The expat communities in Vietnam keep on growing.” Moreover, tourism is increasing every year, especially the number of returning tourists

    HCMC FAMOUS DISHES

    Coffee:

    “Cà phê sữa đá” (iced milk coﬀee) is a dish that can easily win the heart of most people in Saigon. There are many ways to enjoy the taste of Vietnamese coﬀee. You can sit in a beautiful, romantic, unique café or simply sit among the busy streets of Saigon with a sidewalk coﬀee on Han Thuyen Street, a favorite among young locals.

    Not only a dish, the bell sound from Hu Tiu stands is one of the signature sounds of the streets of Saigon. You can fnd Hu Tiu anywhere and every place has its own taste.
